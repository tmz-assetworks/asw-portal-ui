GLOSSARY OF TERMS add remove

TERMS

  1. Charging Station Management System (CSMS) : This is the software used by charging networks to manage EV charging stations- in this case AssetWorks FuelFocusEV.
  2. Charging Level : The amount of electric power that an EV receives during charging, typically measured in kilowatts (kW). In North America, Level 2 or AC is the most common for fleets though DC Fast Charger is needed for fast charging and required for many kinds of medium- and heavy-duty trucks.
  3. Charging Rate : The amount of electric power that an EV is receiving during charging, typically measured in kilowatts per hour (kWh). Charging rate can vary based on not just charging hardware and software, but the vehicle itself.
  4. Charging Protocol : The set of rules and standards that determine how an EV communicates with a charging station during charging. AssetWorks uses the industry-standard Open Charge Point Protocol (OCPP).
  5. Level 1 Charging : A type of EV charging that uses a standard 120-volt household outlet and provides a charging rate of 1.4 to 2.3 kW.
  6. Level 2 Charging : A type of EV charging that uses a 240-volt outlet, such as a dryer or oven outlet, and provides a charging rate of 3.3 to 19.2 kW.
  7. DC Fast Charging : A type of EV charging that uses a high-voltage DC power source and provides faster charging. DC Fast Chargers can range from 25k to 350kw or more.
  8. AC Charging : A type of EV charging that uses alternating current (AC) to charge an EV's battery.
  9. DC Charging : A type of EV charging that uses direct current (DC) to charge an EV's battery.
  10. Charging Network : A network of EV charging stations that are connected and managed by a single provider- in this case AssetWorks.
  11. Charging Station Access Card : A RFID card or key fob that is used to authorize EV charging sessions.
  12. Connector Types
    • J1172 : This connector is standard in North America for Level 2 charging.
    • CCS1 : This connector is standard in North America for DC Fast charging.
  13. kWh : kilowatt-hour
  14. VIN :Vehicle Identification Number
  15. RFID : Radio Frequency Identification
  16. EV : Electric Vehicle
  17. MT : Metric Ton
  18. MAC : Media Access Control Address
  19. ID : Identification
